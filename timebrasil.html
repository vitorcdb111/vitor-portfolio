Time Brasil

Released: 2024

Studio: Oak3 Game Studio / Hermit Crab Game Studio

Platforms: Mobile

Skill Focus: Game Design

Time on Project: ~2 months

Team size: 10 people

Engine: Unity

PlayStore: https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.hermitcrabstudio.f2p.gameplaycob

Play mini games: https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.hermitcrabstudio.f2p.gameplaycob

WHAT IS THE PROJECT?

Time Brasil Gameplay is a mobile gaming platform built around a collection of mini games inspired by Olympic sports and the Brazilian national team.

Developed by Hermit Crab, the platform was created in partnership with the Brazilian Olympic Committee to promote Brazilian athletes during the Olympic Games.

Players can compete in different sports-themed mini games, earn points, unlock skins, and engage with a shared progression system, similar to platforms like Roblox or Fortnite, but fully focused on Olympic disciplines and Brazilian identity.

Oak3 Game Studio collaborated on the project by designing and developing three mini games within this ecosystem, following the platform’s technical, visual, and gameplay guidelines.

PROJECT CONTEXT

The project began when one of my partners and I reached out to Hermit Crab after hearing they were working on a larger initiative involving multiple mini games.

We scheduled a conversation with Matheus, their CEO, presented Oak3’s previous work, and shared our interest in contributing to the project.

The conversation flowed naturally, and Hermit Crab saw value in bringing Oak3 into the development process. As a starting point, we agreed that Oak3 would design and develop three mini games, allowing them to evaluate both the structure of their mini-game platform and our collaboration within it.

At the time, Hermit Crab was developing a mobile platform of mini games in partnership with the Brazilian Olympic Committee.

The goal was to promote Brazilian athletes during the Olympic Games through interactive experiences. Players could engage with different Olympic sports, earn points, unlock skins, and progress through the platform, following a structure similar to games like Roblox or Fortnite, but fully centered on Olympic disciplines and the Brazilian national team.

PRODUCTION SETUP

The production was structured around a two-week pipeline per mini game.

Each cycle consisted of one week of development followed by one week dedicated to QA and polishing.

The only exception was the first mini game, which included an additional onboarding week focused on understanding the shared project architecture and the asset creation guidelines defined by Hermit Crab.

This structure was essential to maintain consistency across the platform while working under tight deadlines and allowing multiple teams to collaborate effectively.

From Hermit Crab’s side, the project was supported by a Producer, along with leads in programming, game design, and art direction, who provided guidance, feedback, and validation throughout development.

This ensured that all mini games followed the same technical standards, visual identity, and gameplay principles across the platform.

On Oak3’s side, the core team consisted of myself as Game Designer, a Producer, an Artist, and a Programmer.

In addition, one of my former partners supported the project acting as QA, helping validate gameplay mechanics and design decisions during development.

This setup allowed us to move quickly while staying aligned with the platform’s technical constraints, shared architecture, and overall creative direction.

MY ROLE

On this project, I worked as a Game Designer, acting as the bridge between concept, technical feasibility, and implementation within a very tight production schedule.

For the first two mini games, Hermit Crab provided the initial concepts and a set of guidelines that needed to be followed. While there was room to suggest adjustments and improvements, the main goal was to respect the proposed direction and adapt it to our production constraints and platform limitations.

The first mini game was based on cycling, designed as a bike endless runner.

The second focused on archery.

For the third mini game, Oak3 was given full creative freedom, and we proposed an obstacle racing game inspired by classic Olympic-style mini games.

For the initial mini games, my role involved reviewing the concepts and guidelines, discussing feasibility and scope with the programmer, and translating those ideas into mechanics that could be safely developed within the two-week pipeline.

Based on the progress of the first mini game, I worked closely with my partner and our producer to identify ways to streamline development and reduce risk for the following cycles.

While the second mini game was in development, I began designing the third one from scratch. Given the tight schedule and our growing familiarity with the platform, we deliberately chose a concept that allowed us to reuse systems and knowledge from the previous mini games while still offering a distinct experience.

Classic references like Mario and Sonic Olympic mini games strongly influenced this decision.

My design approach throughout the project was straightforward: respect the proposed concepts, adapt them to our constraints, and focus on making each mini game simple, responsive, and fun.

During development, I worked closely with the team on gameplay feel, balance, and polish, adding juiciness where possible to elevate the experience.

Despite the short scope typical of mini games, this process required constant decision-making to balance ambition and feasibility.

The result was a set of mini games that were easy to pick up, enjoyable to play, and well received by the Hermit Crab team.

REFLECTION

Developing Time Brasil Gameplay was a very different experience from what we were used to at Oak3.

Not only because of the tight scope and the need for fast, precise decisions, but mainly because this project was not about building a standalone game.

Instead, it required designing well-defined mini games that needed to fit seamlessly into a larger platform with pre-established rules and directions.

Hermit Crab’s team was always open and supportive, providing guidance whenever needed.

Since Oak3 was the first external studio to develop mini games for the platform, we also faced situations where documentation was still incomplete or certain decisions had not yet been fully defined.

While this occasionally slowed down development, it never affected the quality of collaboration, and the relationship between the teams remained very positive throughout the project.

Working closely with an experienced game designer from Hermit Crab, who regularly reviewed my work, was another major learning opportunity.

Having direct feedback from someone with deep industry experience helped me grow significantly and refine my design decisions.

It was a genuine pleasure to collaborate with one of the leading game studios in Brazil.

In the end, Hermit Crab decided to temporarily pause the project after evaluating that the platform was not yet ready for a full commercial release.

As a result, most of the mini games developed during this phase are not publicly available.

Despite that, the project was a valuable experience that strengthened my ability to design within constraints, collaborate across studios, and contribute effectively to larger production ecosystems.
